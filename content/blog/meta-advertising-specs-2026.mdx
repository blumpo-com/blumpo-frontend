### 3.2 Video Ads

**Specifications:**

- Formats: MP4, MOV.
- Max file size: often up to 4 GB for many placements.
- Recommended resolution: at least 1080√ó1080 px; vertical or square for mobile.
- Aspect ratios: 1:1, 4:5, 9:16 (especially for Stories/Reels).
- Duration: for Stories/Reels typically under 15‚Äì30 seconds recommended.

**Advantages:**

- Strong storytelling capability, eye-catching motion draws attention.
- Effective for awareness and engagement objectives.

**Disadvantages:**

- Requires more production resources.
- If not optimized for mobile or placement, may underperform.

---

### 3.3 Carousel Ads

**Specifications:**

- Multiple cards: often 2‚Äì10 images/videos per ad.
- Recommended card size/resolution: typically 1080√ó1080 px or larger.
- Aspect ratio often 1:1 for consistency in feed.

**Advantages:**

- Flexible to showcase multiple products, features, or step-by-step.
- Good for traffic and conversion-oriented campaigns.

**Disadvantages:**

- Needs multiple creatives, higher asset production.
- Visual consistency across cards is critical for performance.

---

### 3.4 Stories / Reels / Full-Screen Vertical Ads

**Specifications:**

- Aspect ratio: 9:16 (vertical full screen) for Stories/Reels.
- Image ads: resolution such as 1080√ó1920 px recommended.
- Video max file size: ~4 GB; length often capped around 15‚Äì60 seconds depending on placement.

**Advantages:**

- Mobile-first; occupies full screen, high-impact.
- Appears in immersive placements; good for brand awareness and engagement.

**Disadvantages:**

- Must be formatted and designed specifically for vertical mobile view.
- Less ideal for desktop or very wide format placements.

## 4. Format vs Device: Mobile vs Desktop Strategy

<Image src={Meta_20advertising_20specs_20_2026} alt="blue-phone-case-and-eyeglasses-on-wooden-table.jpg" />/blue-phone-case-and-eyeglasses-on-wooden-table.jpg)

- Mobile usage dominates: most Meta users access via mobile apps. Vertical/portrait formats capture more screen space.
- On desktop, wider formats (square or landscape) still matter, but mobile-optimized assets often drive better ROI.
- **Recommendation:** Always include at least two formats: one optimized for mobile (vertical/4:5/9:16) and one for feed/desktop (1:1 or horizontal) to test performance variations.
